Interactive::House Saints

Hala Alyan

I want the miracle that makes me ordinary

to kiss




the back of her hand. I

pray to the rain when I

pray and I pray to open

and name what surfaces


My favorite house is my

mother. The heart

muffled like a speaker.

There are no gardens


here. Only another year.

The tree leaves like open

palms, up then out then

waiting to be patted.

to resurrect




into a forked river. The

mountain saints are gone


and it's a new country now

The women fold the desert


into a blanket. Dear cross-

stitch pattern of birds. Dear


patron saint of hyphenates.


Of lentils and clean feet. Of

borders and their seven


names. Of returning and tap

water and bismillah.

to leave




with the


blackbirds.


The mediterranean is

an accent.


The Nile is


an accent. My father is

an accent. His Gaza


with an opera.

The saint sees cinder in the tea leaves. The saint turns a daughter into

blue


and the pollution is blue

and the smoke from our

mouths is blue and each

cracked eggs is morning.


The saint touches the

welt and it turns

rhinestone. The saint

says return to your


grandmother, she's

left the door unlocked.

The saint says she's left

you the ovary

crickets


outside the window.


The dead are not dead.

They are pinned to each


siren--hot pink,

green, white, white--like


breaks to a wire.

It is not enough


to say love in Arabic.

You must say


be the thing that buries me,

a door


from spackled sky to

spackled room.


You don't need a house


to spread out your rug.

Maybe loz trees and


peach trees and a rooster


that wakes up every

morning,


a throat or tongue

that turns the clock back to its first hour.
